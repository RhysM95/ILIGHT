User Guide

In this busy world, who's got the time to get up, walk to the light switch, turn the light on, walk back to your seat... So much effort, there's got to be an easier way! Well now there is, introducing the iLIGHT Monitoring and controlling the lights in your house or office building has never been easier! With this system you will know the power draw, brightness and have the ability to turn each light on or off right from your fingertips.

The first page you will encounter is the account registration page. This page is for you to create a personal login to view your light data.

  • Step 1: Enter a username, this username will define your lights so you view yours and yours alone.
  • Step 2: Enter a password, this should be complex but something you will remember.
  • Step 3: Re-enter your password, this should match the above password. There will be an error if it doesn't.
  • Step 4: Click Register
  • Registration

This page is for you to login to your existing account

  • Step 1: Enter your username, this username is the one you entered on the registration page.
  • Step 2: Enter your password.
  • Step 3: Click "login", there will be an error if you have entered the wrong username or password.
  • Login

This page will alert you to any detections over or under the limits.

  • Object name: This is the light that the alarm is being triggered for.
  • Alarm description: This is the type of alarm being triggered. (Over-range, under-range, etc.)
  • Time logged: This is the time that the alarm was triggered.
  • Value: This is the value that was taken from the light that has triggered the alarm.
  • Priority: This is a value between 1 and 5 that will show how critical this alarm is and how much attention it needs. 1 being critical and needs to be looked at immediately and 5 being not urgent.
  • Alarms

This page is to see all the lights you have registered to your account. Each light can be clicked on to view current operational data.

  • Each light that is registered will be able to be viewed on this page.
  • The lights are able to be viewed individually by clicking on the light you would like to see more information on.
  • Once in the specific light you will be able to see all current data on that light.
  • Lights

This page will let you control your system as a whole

  • Restart: This will restart the whole system.
  • Off/ On: This will enable you to be able to switch the whole system off or on.
  • Auto/ Manual: This switches the whole system between automatic and manual control.
